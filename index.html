Building Trust

A brief presentation on the important aspects of working with clients and keeping them happy

Communicating with the Client

Before I go on trust - establish best practices on communicating with clients

Speak Their Language

Technical Background?

Don't overwhelm non technical clients with technicalities - always explain with estimates and consequences

Be precise and technical where appropriate

Be Explicit

Record all requirements on stories or tickets and minimise verbal task agreements

Choose a medium suitable for the client's technical abilities - i.e. Github <=> Trello

Spell out the requirements

  • list task goals
  • link to design or formal requirements
  • state acceptance criteria

Communicate Often

Keep client updated on a daily basis. Even better: small updates multiple times a day

Use tools that notify clients as you work

  • Github
  • Pivotal Tracker
  • Jira
  • Trello

Formalise Acceptance

A task is complete if

  • fulfils the stated acceptance criteria
  • is accepted by the client - iterate until the client accepts

Watch out for scope creep

How to Build Trust?

Four simple steps to build trust with clients

Reliability

  • don't be late - let the client know if you are
  • be consistent
  • don't surprise
  • keep the client informed

Sincerity

  • be honest - don't undertake what can't be realistically delivered
  • don't just say yes - refusing a request is better than accepting and failing

Competence

  • only accept technically feasible tasks
  • delegate tasks to the most appropriate people

Empathy

  • embrace the client's prospective: good and bad. Negative client assessments are free hints for improvement
  • care for the client, their vision and success
  • client should feel that we work exclusively for them
  • advance the client; not yourself
